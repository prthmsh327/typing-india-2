HOW THE CPCT PENALTY FORMULA WORKS

Most typing tests just give you a WPM score based on correct words. CPCT is stricter. Here's exactly how your CPCT score is calculated:

Step 1 — Gross WPM: Total correct characters typed ÷ 5 ÷ minutes elapsed

Step 2 — Penalty: Number of incorrect words × 0.5

Step 3 — Net WPM: Gross WPM − Penalty

Example: You type 200 correct characters and make 6 errors in 5 minutes. Gross WPM = (200÷5)÷5 = 8 WPM... in 15 minutes: Gross WPM = (1500÷5)÷15 = 20 WPM. Penalty = 6 × 0.5 = 3. Net WPM = 20 − 3 = 17 WPM. ❌ Below the 30 WPM threshold.

This is why accuracy matters more than raw speed in CPCT. One burst of 10 fast errors costs you 5 WPM — which can be the difference between pass and fail.

CPCT 2025 EXAM SCHEDULE & ELIGIBILITY

CPCT is conducted by MAP_IT (Madhya Pradesh Agency for Promotion of Information Technology) multiple times per year. Exam dates are announced on the official CPCT portal at cpct.mp.gov.in.

Eligibility: 12th pass from a recognized board. No age bar for general practice tests. Age limit applies per the specific government post you are applying for.

Certificate validity: 7 years from date of issue. You can retake to get a better score at any time.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Does this simulator use the exact CPCT penalty formula?
Yes. We apply the official formula: Net WPM = Gross WPM − (0.5 × number of errors). The gross WPM, penalty deduction, and net WPM are all shown live as you type so you can see exactly how errors affect your score in real time.
Are the passages similar to the actual CPCT exam?
Our passages are written to match CPCT passage style — formal English/Hindi text covering civic, general knowledge, and administrative topics. The actual CPCT uses different passages each attempt, but the difficulty level and word complexity are calibrated to match.
What keyboard should I use for CPCT Hindi typing?
CPCT Hindi typing uses the Inscript keyboard layout with Mangal font. Set up Inscript on Windows via Settings → Time & Language → Language → Hindi → Options → Add Inscript keyboard. This is free and available on all Windows computers. Do not use Krutidev or Remington layout — these are not accepted in CPCT.
I scored 30 WPM gross but failed. Why?
If you made errors, your net WPM will be below 30. For example, 30 gross WPM with 10 errors = 30 − 5 = 25 net WPM, which is a fail. You need 30 NET WPM — meaning your gross speed must be high enough to absorb the penalty from your errors. Aim for 35+ gross WPM to safely clear 30 net WPM.
How many times can I take CPCT?
There is no limit on CPCT attempts. You can take the exam as many times as needed. The exam is conducted multiple times a year. Only your latest valid certificate (within 7 years) is considered by government departments.
