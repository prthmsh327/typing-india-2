SSC CHSL TYPING TEST FORMAT

SSC CHSL (Combined Higher Secondary Level) Skill Test for LDC (Lower Division Clerk) posts requires a typing speed of 35 WPM in English or 30 WPM in Hindi. The test duration is 10 minutes. Unlike CPCT, SSC does not apply a penalty for errors — instead, accuracy is assessed separately and candidates with very low accuracy may be disqualified.

The typing test in SSC is qualifying in nature for most posts — you need to clear it to proceed, but ranks are determined by the written exam score.

SSC CGL DEO TYPING FORMAT

SSC CGL Data Entry Operator posts require 15,000 Key Depressions Per Hour (KDPH) in English only. This is approximately 50 WPM. The test is conducted on a computer with actual data entry software. Practice by targeting 50+ WPM in our Hard difficulty mode.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How is SSC CHSL typing test different from CPCT?
SSC CHSL does not use a penalty formula like CPCT. Your gross WPM is your final score. SSC assesses accuracy separately — extremely low accuracy can disqualify you, but there's no WPM deduction per error. This makes SSC more forgiving than CPCT for candidates who make occasional mistakes.
Can I use Hindi typing for SSC CHSL?
Yes. SSC CHSL allows either English (35 WPM) or Hindi (30 WPM) typing. The choice is made at the time of application. Note that the Hindi test uses Inscript keyboard layout. If you are comfortable with Hindi Inscript, the lower WPM threshold (30 vs 35) can be an advantage.
Is the SSC typing test conducted online or offline?
The SSC Skill Test (typing) is conducted at SSC regional offices on dedicated computer terminals. It is not taken online from home. The computer setup, keyboard, and software are provided by SSC. Practice on a standard QWERTY keyboard for best preparation.
