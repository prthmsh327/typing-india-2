HOW TO CHOOSE WHICH EXAM TO PREPARE FOR

If you're in Madhya Pradesh or Chhattisgarh: CPCT is your first priority. Nearly all state government jobs require it, and the certificate is valid for 7 years across multiple departments.

If you're targeting central government jobs (SSC): Practice English typing at 35+ WPM. SSC CHSL is the most accessible central government typing exam. Start with our SSC practice mode.

If you're in Uttar Pradesh: UP Lekhpal and UP Police clerk require Hindi typing at 25 WPM using Inscript. Learn Inscript keyboard first — it takes 4–6 weeks — then focus on speed.

If you're in Rajasthan: Patwari exam is your target. 25 WPM Hindi with 80% accuracy is more achievable than CPCT's 85% accuracy requirement.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is CPCT valid for SSC and central government jobs?
No. CPCT is specifically for Madhya Pradesh state government jobs. SSC conducts its own separate typing test at regional offices for CHSL and other posts. You need to clear both separately if applying for both MP state and central government posts.
Can I use the same typing practice for all government exams?
Mostly yes for English exams — the QWERTY keyboard and passage types are similar. For Hindi typing, all state exams now use Inscript layout, so one practice method works across all states. The difference is mainly in the WPM threshold and accuracy requirement per exam.
Which exam has the easiest typing requirement?
Rajasthan Patwari requires 25 WPM Hindi with 80% accuracy — the most achievable threshold among major state government exams. SSC CHSL has no penalty formula, making it more forgiving than CPCT at the same WPM level.
What is the difference between WPM and KDPH?
WPM (Words Per Minute) counts each group of 5 characters as one word. KDPH (Key Depressions Per Hour) counts every single keystroke including spaces. SSC CGL DEO post uses KDPH: 15,000 KDPH = 15,000 ÷ 60 minutes ÷ 5 chars per word = 50 WPM approximately.
