WHAT IS CPCT?

CPCT (Computer Proficiency Certification Test) is conducted by the Madhya Pradesh Agency for Promotion of Information Technology (MAP_IT) for assessing the computer skills of candidates for government posts in Madhya Pradesh. Clearing CPCT is mandatory for many Group B and Group C government jobs in MP.

The typing component of CPCT tests your ability to type English or Hindi text accurately within a time limit. The standard requires 30 WPM in English or 20 WPM in Hindi with a minimum accuracy of 85%.

CPCT EXAM PATTERN 2025

Duration: 2 hours total (Computer knowledge + Typing)

Typing test duration: 15 minutes per language

Scoring: Gross speed minus penalty for errors = Net speed

Penalty: Each error deducts 0.5 WPM from gross speed

Keyboard: English — standard QWERTY. Hindi — Inscript layout, Mangal font

Pass criteria: Minimum net speed AND minimum 85% accuracy — both must be met

CPCT Preparation Tips

Practice daily for 30–45 minutes using passages similar to actual CPCT content. Focus on accuracy over speed — a single error in CPCT carries a 0.5 WPM penalty. Use this test's 5-minute and 15-minute modes to simulate actual exam pressure. Review your most common error patterns after each test.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many times can I appear for CPCT exam?
There is no limit on the number of attempts for the CPCT exam. You can appear as many times as needed. The exam is conducted multiple times a year by MAP_IT. Your best valid score (within validity period) is considered for government job applications.
What is the validity of CPCT certificate?
A CPCT certificate is valid for 7 years from the date of issue. After 7 years, you need to appear again to renew your certification if you want to apply for government jobs requiring CPCT clearance.
Is CPCT required for all MP government jobs?
CPCT is mandatory for Group B and Group C posts in Madhya Pradesh state government that involve computer usage. It is typically required for positions like Assistant Grade, Data Entry Operator, Clerk, and similar posts across all MP government departments.
Can I practice on a phone or tablet?
We recommend practicing on a desktop or laptop computer only — specifically the same type of keyboard you will use in the actual CPCT exam. Mobile typing is fundamentally different from keyboard typing and will not help your CPCT preparation.
